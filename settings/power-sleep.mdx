LongFred runs on battery power and includes several features designed to extend runtime and protect your session. The throttle screen displays a live battery level icon, and the device can enter deep sleep automatically or on demand to conserve power when not in use. A built-in WiThrottle heartbeat keeps your server connection alive, and a dedicated save action lets you preserve your current locomotive acquisitions across power cycles.

## Battery indicator

The battery level is shown as an icon in the corner of the throttle screen. LongFred samples the battery voltage through the ADC every **10 seconds**, averaging 20 readings for a stable result. You can cycle through three display modes by pressing the **ShowHideBattery** action button:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Icon" icon="battery-half">
    A battery icon is shown on the throttle screen, representing the current charge level graphically. This is the default display mode.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Icon + Percent" icon="battery-full">
    The battery icon is shown alongside a numeric percentage, e.g. `78%`. The most informative mode.
  </Card>
  <Card title="None" icon="eye-slash">
    The battery icon is hidden. Useful if you prefer a cleaner screen and know your battery is healthy.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

Press **ShowHideBattery** repeatedly to cycle through **Icon → Icon + Percent → None → Icon →** …

<Note>
  The battery display mode is session-only and resets to the firmware default on reboot. The default mode shows the battery icon without a percentage figure.
</Note>

## Sleep modes

LongFred supports three ways to enter **deep sleep**, a low-power state that shuts down the WiFi radio and processor core:

<Accordion title="Manual sleep (Extras → 7 OFF / Sleep)">
  The most common way to put LongFred to sleep. Navigate to **Extras** from the main menu and press **7 OFF / Sleep**. A warning screen appears for **2 seconds**, giving you time to cancel by pressing Back. After the countdown, the device enters deep sleep.

  To wake the device, press the **encoder button** (the push-action on the rotary encoder). LongFred will reboot, reconnect to WiFi, and restore your saved locos if any were saved.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Auto-sleep on inactivity (disabled by default)">
  LongFred can enter deep sleep automatically after a configurable period of inactivity — defined as having no active WiThrottle server connection. This feature is disabled by default in the standard firmware build.

  When enabled, the same 2-second warning screen appears before sleep triggers.

  <Info>
    Auto-sleep on inactivity is a **compile-time setting** and cannot be changed from the UI. Contact your firmware provider if you need to enable or adjust the inactivity timeout.
  </Info>
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Auto-sleep on low battery (disabled by default)">
  LongFred can also enter deep sleep when the battery percentage drops below a set threshold. This feature is disabled by default in the standard firmware build.

  When triggered, the display shows the "Battery low" message for 2 seconds before entering deep sleep.

  <Info>
    The low-battery sleep threshold is a **compile-time setting** and cannot be changed from the UI. Contact your firmware provider if you need to enable or adjust this threshold.
  </Info>
</Accordion>

## Waking from deep sleep

Press the **encoder button** (the push-action on the rotary encoder) to wake LongFred from deep sleep. The device performs a full reboot — it reconnects to WiFi, rediscovers your WiThrottle server, and automatically reacquires any locos that were saved before sleeping.

<Warning>
  Deep sleep is a **full power-off state**. Any locomotive acquisitions that were not saved (see below) will be lost. Use **Extras → 9 Save Locos** before sleeping if you want them restored on wake.
</Warning>

## WiThrottle heartbeat

The WiThrottle protocol requires a periodic keepalive message to prevent the server from dropping idle clients. LongFred sends this heartbeat automatically in the background. You can toggle it on or off during a session:

- Navigate to **Extras → 3 Heartbt Tgl**
- Each press toggles the heartbeat between enabled and disabled
- The current heartbeat state is visible as a small indicator on the throttle screen

<Note>
  Disabling the heartbeat is intended for advanced users and debugging. Most WiThrottle servers (including JMRI and DCC-EX) will disconnect a client that stops sending keepalives after a short timeout. Re-enable the heartbeat with another press of **3 Heartbt Tgl** if you lose your server connection.
</Note>

## Saving locomotive acquisitions

When LongFred reconnects to a WiThrottle server after a reboot or sleep cycle, it can automatically re-acquire the locomotives you had previously selected. This requires saving the current acquisitions to non-volatile storage first.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Extras menu">
    From the **Throttle** screen, open the main menu and navigate to **Extras**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save locos">
    Press **9 Save Locos**. LongFred writes the current loco acquisitions (up to 12 slots) to non-volatile storage immediately. A brief confirmation message appears on screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reconnect or wake">
    After the next reboot, WiFi reconnect, or wake from deep sleep, LongFred reads the saved acquisitions and automatically reacquires them from the server.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Make it a habit to press **9 Save Locos** before putting LongFred to sleep or walking away from the layout. Up to 12 locomotive acquisitions across all throttle slots can be saved in a single action.
</Tip>
